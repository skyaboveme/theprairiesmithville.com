Breaking Down the Numbers: What You Actually Save

Let's get specific about what living in each community actually costs and what you get for your money.

Home Prices: Your Biggest Investment

Housing costs are where you'll see the most dramatic differences between these three communities:

Smithville (The Prairie)

  • Median Home Price: $357,000
  • Price Per Square Foot: $222
  • What You Get: Brand new 3-4 bedroom homes (1,400-1,800 sq ft) with modern finishes, energy-efficient features, yards, and 2-car garages
  • Monthly Payment (20% down): $2,200-$2,500 (including taxes and insurance)

Austin

  • Median Home Price: $450,000-$550,000+
  • Price Per Square Foot: $294
  • What You Get: Often older homes (20-40 years) needing updates, smaller lots, limited parking, 1,200-1,600 sq ft
  • Monthly Payment (20% down): $3,200-$4,000+ (including taxes and insurance)

Bastrop

  • Median Home Price: $378,000
  • Price Per Square Foot: $172-$203
  • What You Get: Mix of older and newer homes, variable quality, 1,400-1,700 sq ft
  • Monthly Payment (20% down): $2,400-$2,700 (including taxes and insurance)

The Smithville Advantage: Save $1,000-$1,500 per month on housing compared to Austin, while getting a newer, larger home with better amenities.

Property Taxes: The Hidden Cost That Adds Up

Property taxes are where Smithville really shines. Here's what you'll actually pay annually on a typical home:

Smithville Example:

  • Home Value: $357,000
  • Tax Rate: 1.25%
  • Annual Property Tax: $4,463
  • Monthly: $372

Austin Example:

  • Home Value: $500,000 (comparable home)
  • Tax Rate: 1.63%
  • Annual Property Tax: $8,150
  • Monthly: $679

Bastrop Example:

  • Home Value: $378,000
  • Tax Rate: 1.33%-1.39%
  • Annual Property Tax: $5,027-$5,254
  • Monthly: $419-$438

Annual Tax Savings: Living in Smithville saves you $3,687 per year compared to Austin, and $564-$791 compared to Bastrop!

Commute & Airport Access: Getting Where You Need to Go

Accessibility matters, especially if you work in Austin or travel frequently. Here's the real story on commute times:

Austin-Bergstrom International Airport

  • Smithville: 37-47 minutes via Highway 71 (mostly highway driving, no traffic stress)
  • Austin (varies by location): 20-30 minutes from central Austin, but often 45+ minutes during rush hour due to severe traffic congestion
  • Bastrop: 35-45 minutes via Highway 71

The Reality: While Austin is technically closer to the airport, Smithville and Bastrop residents often reach the airport in similar or even less time because they avoid Austin's notorious traffic congestion. Smithville offers a stress-free, predictable drive on Highway 71 with no tolls.

Commuting to Downtown Austin

  • Smithville: 45 minutes via Highway 71 (mostly smooth highway driving)
  • Bastrop: 40-50 minutes depending on route and traffic
  • Austin Suburbs (Round Rock, Cedar Park): 30-60 minutes in heavy stop-and-go traffic

Many Smithville residents report that their 45-minute highway commute is actually less stressful and more predictable than Austin residents' shorter-distance but traffic-heavy commutes. Plus, with the rise of hybrid work, many people only commute 2-3 days per week.

Daily Traffic Stress

Austin Traffic Statistics (2025):

  • Ranked #15 worst traffic in the United States
  • Average commute time: 28 minutes (up 2.9% from last year)
  • 4 hours and 50 minutes of weekday traffic congestion
  • I-35, MoPac, and Highway 183 are severely congested daily
  • Population expected to double by 2050, making traffic worse

Smithville Traffic Reality:

  • Virtually no traffic congestion within town
  • Average local commute: 15 minutes
  • Stress-free errands, school drop-offs, and daily activities
  • Kids can safely walk or bike in neighborhoods
  • Quality of life dramatically improved without traffic stress

Activities & Quality of Life: What's There to Do?

Cost of living isn't just about money—it's about quality of life. Here's how each community stacks up:

Outdoor Recreation & Nature

Smithville:

  • Colorado River access for fishing, kayaking, and water sports
  • Buescher State Park (minutes away) for hiking and camping
  • Historic downtown with local shops and restaurants
  • Community events: Jamboree, Christmas on Main Street
  • Less crowded, authentic outdoor experiences
  • Film location for "Hope Floats" and other movies

Austin:

  • More entertainment options (music venues, restaurants, nightlife)
  • Parks and trails (but often crowded)
  • Cultural attractions (museums, theaters)
  • Professional sports and large events
  • Higher cost for activities and dining

Bastrop:

  • Bastrop State Park and Lost Pines area
  • Historic downtown district
  • Colorado River activities
  • Growing restaurant and shop scene
  • Community festivals and events

Schools & Education

Smithville ISD Advantages:

  • Small class sizes mean teachers know every student
  • Strong community support for schools
  • Personalized education with individual attention
  • Safe, walkable schools within the community
  • Excellent teacher-to-student ratios
  • Active parent involvement

Austin schools vary dramatically by area, with top-rated schools often in the most expensive neighborhoods. Bastrop schools are good but growing rapidly, leading to larger class sizes. Smithville offers consistent, quality education with the small-town advantage of personal attention.

Safety & Community

  • Smithville: Very low crime rate, neighbors know each other, safe for kids to play outside, strong community bonds
  • Austin: Crime varies widely by neighborhood, higher overall rates, less community connection in many areas
  • Bastrop: Low to moderate crime, growing community, mix of rural and suburban feel

Real-Life Scenarios: Who Chooses What?

Choose Smithville if you:

  • Want maximum home for your money
  • Value small-town safety and community
  • Work remotely or hybrid (2-3 days in office)
  • Don't mind 45-minute commute for significant savings
  • Have or plan to have children (excellent schools)
  • Want new construction with modern amenities
  • Prioritize outdoor recreation and nature
  • Desire lower stress and better work-life balance

Choose Austin if you:

  • Must be within 15 minutes of downtown office
  • Prioritize nightlife, dining, and entertainment above all
  • Don't have or want children (schools less important)
  • Can afford $500K+ for housing
  • Don't mind traffic and urban congestion
  • Want to walk/bike everywhere (specific neighborhoods only)

Choose Bastrop if you:

  • Want balance between small-town and city access
  • Like outdoor recreation (state parks)
  • Can handle mixed-quality housing stock
  • Don't need new construction
  • Want slightly shorter commute than Smithville
  • Okay with moderate property taxes

After analyzing every angle—housing costs, property taxes, commute times, traffic, schools, safety, and quality of life—Smithville emerges as the clear winner for families seeking value and livability in Central Texas.

Smithville's Winning Formula:

  • Best Value: Save $16,620/year compared to Austin
  • Lowest Taxes: 1.25% property tax rate (vs 1.63% Austin, 1.39% Bastrop)
  • New Homes: The Prairie offers brand new construction with warranties
  • Top Schools: Small class sizes and personalized education
  • Safest Community: Very low crime, friendly neighbors
  • No Traffic Stress: Zero congestion for daily life
  • Easy Commute: 45 minutes to Austin on smooth Highway 71

For families who want to own a beautiful new home, provide their children with excellent education, and save thousands of dollars each year—all while staying within reach of Austin's opportunities—Smithville is the smart choice.
