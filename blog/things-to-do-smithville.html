10 Best Things to Do in Smithville, TX

October 10, 2025 • 7 min read

Considering a move to Smithville? Already living here and looking for new adventures? This charming Texas town offers a surprising array of activities, attractions, and experiences. From historic sites to outdoor recreation, here are the 10 best things to do in Smithville, TX.

1. Explore Historic Downtown Smithville

Downtown Smithville is the heart of the community, where history comes alive on every corner. This beautifully preserved historic district features:

  • Architectural Gems: Buildings dating back to the late 1800s showcase classic Texas architecture
  • Local Shops: Unique boutiques, antique stores, and specialty shops you won't find anywhere else
  • Historic Plaques: Learn about Smithville's rich history as you stroll the streets
  • Photo Opportunities: Picture-perfect storefronts and vintage charm at every turn
  • Events Space: The downtown hosts festivals, car shows, and community celebrations year-round

Don't miss the iconic downtown square where locals gather for community events. The area gained additional fame as a filming location for Hollywood movies, adding to its historic charm.

2. Enjoy Colorado River Adventures

The Colorado River is Smithville's natural playground, offering endless opportunities for water-based recreation:

  • Fishing: Bass, catfish, and other species make this an angler's paradise
  • Kayaking & Canoeing: Peaceful paddles through scenic waterways
  • Tubing: Float the river on hot summer days
  • Swimming: Several access points for taking a refreshing dip
  • Riverside Picnics: Pack a lunch and enjoy riverside dining
  • Wildlife Watching: Spot herons, turtles, and other native wildlife

The river's proximity to town makes it easy to enjoy water activities year-round. Many Prairie Smithville residents spend weekends exploring different sections of this beautiful waterway.

3. Visit the Railroad and Heritage Museum

Smithville's railroad history is an integral part of its identity. The Railroad and Heritage Museum showcases:

  • Historic Exhibits: Artifacts and displays documenting Smithville's railroad heritage
  • Local History: Stories of the families and industries that built this community
  • Educational Programs: Perfect for families and school groups
  • Historic Photographs: See how Smithville looked over the decades
  • Railroad Artifacts: Original equipment and memorabilia from the railway era

Located in downtown Smithville, the museum is free to visit and offers a fascinating glimpse into what made this town thrive.

4. Dine at Local Restaurants

Smithville's restaurant scene punches above its weight, offering diverse dining options for every taste:

  • Vernon's Kuntry Katfish: Legendary fried catfish and Southern comfort food
  • El Progreso: Authentic Tex-Mex in a welcoming family atmosphere
  • Riverside Bar & Grill: Burgers, steaks, and cold drinks with river views
  • Main Street Eateries: Cafes and diners serving breakfast and lunch
  • Food Trucks: Rotating selection of mobile eateries around town
  • BBQ Joints: True Texas barbecue just like you'd expect

Whether you're craving home-style cooking or want to try something new, Smithville's restaurants offer quality food and friendly service that keeps locals and visitors coming back.

5. Spend Time at Riverbend Park

Riverbend Park is Smithville's premier outdoor recreation destination, offering:

  • Playgrounds: Modern equipment for kids of all ages
  • Sports Fields: Baseball, softball, and soccer facilities
  • Picnic Areas: Covered pavilions perfect for family gatherings
  • Walking Trails: Paved paths for walking, jogging, or biking
  • River Access: Direct access to the Colorado River
  • Community Events: Host to concerts, movies in the park, and festivals

The park is a gathering place for families from The Prairie and throughout Smithville. On any given weekend, you'll find youth sports games, birthday parties, and neighbors enjoying the beautiful Texas weather.

6. Tour Hope Floats Filming Locations

Movie fans flock to Smithville to see where Sandra Bullock filmed the beloved 1998 romantic comedy "Hope Floats":

  • Main Street: The central filming location for many iconic scenes
  • Downtown Buildings: Several storefronts featured in the movie
  • Historic Homes: Victorian-era houses visible in background shots
  • Self-Guided Tour: Create your own movie location walking tour
  • Photo Spots: Recreate your favorite scenes from the film

Beyond "Hope Floats," Smithville has appeared in several other films including Terrence Malick's "Tree of Life" and the dark comedy "Bernie." The town's authentic small-town character makes it perfect for filmmakers seeking genuine Texas charm.

7. Attend Community Events

Smithville's strong community spirit shines through its many annual events and celebrations:

  • Smithville Jamboree: Annual celebration with live music, food, and family fun
  • Christmas in Smithville: Holiday lights, parade, and festive activities
  • Farmers Market: Fresh produce, baked goods, and local crafts
  • Car Shows: Classic and custom automobiles on display downtown
  • Independence Day Celebration: Fireworks, BBQ, and patriotic festivities
  • High School Sports: Support the Smithville Tigers at Friday night football games

These events are more than entertainment—they're opportunities to meet neighbors, build friendships, and feel connected to your community. The Prairie residents often organize group attendance at these events.

8. Explore Nearby Attractions

Smithville's central location provides easy access to major Central Texas attractions:

  • Bastrop State Park (20 minutes): Hiking, camping, and the famous Lost Pines forest
  • La Grange (25 minutes): Historic town with excellent restaurants and antique shopping
  • Austin (45 minutes): State capital with unlimited dining, entertainment, and culture
  • Brenham/Blue Bell Creamery (60 minutes): Famous ice cream factory tours
  • Round Top (40 minutes): Antique shows and charming historic town
  • Lake Bastrop (15 minutes): Additional water recreation opportunities

This strategic location means Smithville residents enjoy small-town living with big-city access. Weekend adventures are always within reach.

9. Enjoy Outdoor Recreation

Beyond the river and parks, Smithville offers numerous outdoor activities:

  • Buescher State Park: Just minutes away with hiking, mountain biking, and camping
  • Bird Watching: Part of the Great Texas Birding Trail with numerous species
  • Cycling: Country roads perfect for road cycling with minimal traffic
  • Golf: Nearby courses in Bastrop and La Grange
  • Geocaching: Hunt for hidden treasures throughout the area
  • Star Gazing: Low light pollution means incredible night sky views

The mild Central Texas climate allows for year-round outdoor enjoyment. Many Prairie Smithville residents moved here specifically for the outdoor lifestyle opportunities.

10. Shop Local

Supporting local businesses while finding unique treasures is easy in Smithville:

  • Antique Stores: Multiple shops offering vintage finds and collectibles
  • Gift Boutiques: Unique items perfect for presents or home decor
  • Art Galleries: Local artists showcasing Texas-inspired works
  • Specialty Shops: Bookstores, jewelry stores, and craft supplies
  • Garden Centers: Plants and supplies perfect for Texas landscapes
  • Hardware & Feed Stores: Everything you need for home and property maintenance

Shopping in Smithville means supporting your neighbors and finding items with character and history. The personal service at these locally-owned businesses creates a shopping experience you can't find at big-box stores.

Making the Most of Smithville Living

These ten activities only scratch the surface of what Smithville offers. The real joy of living here comes from the daily experiences:

  • Morning coffee on your back porch listening to birds
  • Evening walks through safe, friendly neighborhoods
  • Spontaneous trips to the river on hot afternoons
  • Knowing your neighbors and being part of a community
  • Short drives to world-class entertainment when desired
