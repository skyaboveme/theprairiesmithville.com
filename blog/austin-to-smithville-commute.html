Living in Smithville, Working in Austin

October 1, 2025 • 8 min read

Want to enjoy Austin's career opportunities without Austin's cost of living? Thousands of professionals have discovered the perfect balance: living in Smithville while working in Austin. Here's everything you need to know about making this lifestyle work for you.

The Commute: What to Expect

The drive from Smithville to Austin is approximately 45 minutes, making it one of the most reasonable commutes in the Austin metro area. Let's break down the details:

Route Options

There are two primary routes from Smithville to Austin:

  • Highway 71 West (Primary Route): The most direct route, taking you straight into Austin
  • TX-21 to Highway 290 (Alternative Route): Useful during construction or accidents on Highway 71

Commute Time Breakdown

  • Morning Commute (6:30-7:30 AM): 40-50 minutes to downtown Austin
  • Morning Commute (7:30-9:00 AM): 45-60 minutes depending on Austin traffic
  • Evening Commute (5:00-6:30 PM): 50-65 minutes, mostly due to Austin city traffic
  • Off-Peak Hours: 35-40 minutes with minimal traffic
  • Weekends: 40-45 minutes with lighter traffic throughout

Distance Details

  • Smithville to East Austin: ~40 miles
  • Smithville to Downtown Austin: ~45 miles
  • Smithville to Northwest Austin: ~55 miles
  • Most commute miles are highway driving (not stop-and-go)

What Makes This Commute Manageable

Unlike many Austin-area commutes, the Smithville route offers several advantages:

  • Highway Driving: Most of your commute is smooth highway, not stressful city traffic
  • Scenic Route: Beautiful Texas countryside makes the drive pleasant
  • Reverse Commute: You're traveling against the worst traffic flow
  • Predictable Timing: Fewer variables than city-based commutes
  • No Tolls: Highway 71 is toll-free

The Financial Advantage: Real Cost Comparison

Let's look at real numbers comparing life in Smithville versus Austin:

Housing Costs

Smithville (The Prairie):

  • New 3BR/2BA home: $316,500-$350,000
  • Approximate monthly payment: $2,200-$2,500 (with 20% down, including taxes/insurance)
  • Property tax rate: Lower than Travis County

Austin (Comparable Home):

  • Similar 3BR/2BA home: $450,000-$550,000+
  • Approximate monthly payment: $3,200-$4,000+ (with 20% down, including taxes/insurance)
  • Often older homes needing updates

Monthly Savings: $1,000-$1,500 on housing alone

Complete Cost Comparison

Housing is just the beginning. Here's a comprehensive look at cost of living differences:

Additional Monthly Expenses:

  • Utilities: $50-75/month less in Smithville (smaller bills, newer efficient homes)
  • Groceries: 10-15% lower at Smithville stores
  • Dining Out: 20-30% less expensive locally
  • Auto Insurance: $20-40/month less (lower rates in smaller towns)
  • Gas: $200-250/month for commuting

Annual Savings Breakdown

Let's calculate the real annual savings of Smithville living:

  • Housing savings: $12,000-$18,000/year
  • Property taxes: $2,000-$3,000/year savings
  • Utilities: $600-$900/year savings
  • Insurance and other: $500-$1,000/year savings
  • Commuting costs: -$2,500-$3,000/year

Net Annual Savings: $12,600-$18,900 per year

Even after accounting for commuting costs (gas, vehicle maintenance, tolls if applicable), families living in Smithville save over $1,000 per month compared to living in Austin proper.

Quality of Life Benefits

The financial savings are compelling, but many Austin commuters say the lifestyle improvements are even more valuable:

More Space for Your Family

  • Larger Homes: Get 1,400-1,800 sq ft for what a tiny Austin condo costs
  • Actual Yards: Fenced backyards where kids and pets can play safely
  • Garage Parking: Two-car garages standard at The Prairie
  • Storage Space: Room for your belongings without paying for storage units
  • New Construction: Modern, move-in ready homes with warranties

Better Environment for Families

  • Excellent Schools: Smithville ISD with small class sizes and dedicated teachers
  • Safe Community: Low crime rates and friendly neighbors
  • Outdoor Recreation: Colorado River, parks, and nature at your doorstep
  • Small-Town Values: Neighbors know each other, community events bring people together
  • Less Traffic Stress: No Austin traffic when running errands or taking kids to activities

Lower Stress Living

  • Come home to peace and quiet after work
  • No fighting Austin traffic for basic errands
  • Kids walk or bike safely in the neighborhood
  • Genuine work-life separation
  • Slower pace that reduces daily stress

Making the Commute Work

Current Smithville residents who commute to Austin share these strategies for success:

Optimize Your Schedule

  • Flexible Hours: If possible, start earlier or later to avoid peak traffic
  • Compressed Workweek: Some commuters work four 10-hour days
  • Hybrid Schedule: Work from home 2-3 days per week if possible
  • Remote Fridays: Even one remote day weekly dramatically improves quality of life

Make Commute Time Productive

  • Audiobooks: "Read" 30-40 books per year during your commute
  • Podcasts: Stay informed, learn new skills, or be entertained
  • Language Learning: Use apps or audio courses during drive time
  • Phone Calls: Catch up with family and friends (hands-free, of course)
  • Decompression Time: Use the drive to mentally transition between work and home

Vehicle Considerations

  • Fuel-efficient vehicle reduces commuting costs
  • Comfortable seats essential for daily drives
  • Good sound system makes entertainment enjoyable
  • Reliable vehicle prevents commute stress
  • Regular maintenance keeps commute smooth

Practical Tips

  • Keep extra clothes at office for unexpected meetings
  • Pack lunch and snacks to save money
  • Download offline content for dead zones
  • Join carpool with other Smithville commuters
  • Learn multiple routes for traffic contingencies

Remote Work Opportunities

Hybrid Work Benefits

  • Best of Both Worlds: Austin job with minimal commuting
  • 2-3 Days Remote: Commute only 2-3 times per week
  • Flexible Schedule: Work when you're most productive
  • Home Office: Prairie homes have space for dedicated office
  • Better Work-Life Balance: More time with family, less time commuting

Full-Time Remote Workers

If you work remotely full-time, Smithville offers incredible value:

  • Austin salary with Smithville cost of living
  • Larger home office space than Austin apartments
  • Peaceful environment for focused work
  • 45 minutes to Austin when needed for meetings
  • Access to coworking spaces in both Smithville and Austin

Living in Smithville while working in Austin isn't just financially smart—it's a lifestyle upgrade. You get:
