Smithville, Texas: A Gigabit City Ready for Business

November 6, 2025 • 7 min read

Smithville, Texas, a charming town nestled in the heart of the Lone Star State, is more than just a pretty face. While its historic downtown and friendly community are well-known, Smithville has also positioned itself as a forward-thinking city with a robust infrastructure ready to support business growth. One of the most significant indicators of this is its status as a Gigabit City, a designation it has held since 2013.

This early adoption of high-speed fiber optic internet showcases Smithville's commitment to providing its residents and businesses with the tools they need to thrive in the digital age. For families considering a move to The Prairie Smithville, this infrastructure advantage means you can enjoy small-town living without sacrificing connectivity or career opportunities.

What Does Being a Gigabit City Mean?

A Gigabit City designation means Smithville has access to fiber optic internet with speeds up to 1 gigabit per second (1,000 megabits per second). To put this in perspective, the average internet speed in the United States is around 200 Mbps. Smithville's gigabit infrastructure provides speeds that are 5 times faster than the national average.

What This Means for You

  • Seamless Remote Work: Video conferences, large file uploads, and cloud-based applications run flawlessly
  • Multiple Users: Entire family can stream, work, and game simultaneously without slowdowns
  • Future-Proof: Infrastructure ready for emerging technologies and increased bandwidth demands
  • Business Operations: Run a home-based business or startup with enterprise-level connectivity
  • Online Learning: Kids can participate in virtual classes and educational programs without interruption
  • Entertainment: 4K streaming, online gaming, and smart home devices all work perfectly

A City Built for Growth

Smithville's official motto, "Ready for Your Business," is more than just a slogan; it's a promise backed by a wealth of resources and amenities. The city's strategic location, just 45 minutes from Austin-Bergstrom International Airport, places it at the "Heart of the Megapolis," offering easy access to major Texas cities like San Antonio, Houston, and Dallas/Fort Worth.

This prime location, combined with its business-friendly environment, makes Smithville an attractive destination for entrepreneurs, remote workers, and established companies alike.

Perfect for Remote Workers and Digital Professionals

In 2025, the ability to work remotely has become essential for many families. Smithville's gigabit internet infrastructure makes it an ideal location for:

  • Tech Professionals: Software developers, designers, and IT consultants who need reliable, fast connectivity
  • Creative Professionals: Video editors, photographers, and graphic designers who handle large files
  • Entrepreneurs: Business owners running e-commerce, consulting firms, or online services
  • Corporate Remote Workers: Professionals with hybrid schedules who need home office infrastructure
  • Freelancers: Writers, marketers, and consultants who serve clients nationwide
  • Telehealth Providers: Medical professionals conducting virtual appointments

Real-World Example:

A software engineer living at The Prairie Smithville can participate in daily video standup meetings with their Austin-based team, upload code to cloud repositories, download large datasets, and stream training videos—all while their spouse works from home and their kids attend virtual tutoring sessions. The gigabit connection handles it all without a hiccup.

More Than Just Internet: A Complete Business Ecosystem

While being a Gigabit City is a significant draw, Smithville offers a comprehensive ecosystem for business success. The city is home to:

Regional Workforce Training Center

Career Tracks provides workforce development and training programs, ensuring a skilled local talent pool for businesses and educational opportunities for residents.

Innovation Center

Smithville is developing an Innovation Center to support entrepreneurs, startups, and small businesses with resources, mentorship, and collaborative workspace.

Healthcare Infrastructure

Smithville Regional Hospital is the only full-service hospital in Bastrop County, providing comprehensive medical care without traveling to larger cities.

Municipal Airport

The municipal airport serves private aviation and business travel needs, rare for a town of Smithville's size.

Industrial Park Development

A new industrial park is under development, attracting businesses and creating local job opportunities.

  • Lower Housing Costs: $12,000-$18,000/year compared to Austin
  • Reduced Commuting: $3,000-$5,000/year in gas, vehicle wear, and tolls (if working hybrid 2-3 days/week)

Meanwhile, you maintain your Austin salary (or competitive remote salary) while enjoying a lower cost of living—the best of both worlds.
