Why Smithville is Texas's Next Boom Town: Development Plans for 2025-2030

November 10, 2025 • 10 min read

The Austin suburb that's quietly becoming Central Texas's smartest investment opportunity

While everyone's watching Austin's explosive growth, savvy homebuyers are discovering what city planners and developers already know: Smithville is positioned to become Central Texas's next major growth story. Located just 42 miles southeast of Austin, this historic town isn't just riding Austin's coattails—it's creating its own momentum with strategic development plans that promise to transform the community while preserving its small-town charm.

The Numbers Tell the Story

Smithville's population has grown 18% since 2020, outpacing state averages. But that's just the beginning. City planning documents project a 40% population increase by 2030, driven by several major catalysts that are already in motion.

What makes Smithville different from other Austin suburbs? Strategic, sustainable growth backed by infrastructure investments that ensure the town can handle its transformation without losing what makes it special.

Major Development Projects Already Underway

The Highway 71 Corridor Expansion

The Texas Department of Transportation's $150 million investment in Highway 71 improvements isn't just about adding lanes—it's about transforming Smithville's connectivity to Austin. Expected completion by 2027 will reduce commute times by 15-20 minutes, making Smithville more attractive to Austin professionals seeking affordable housing options.

Highway 71 Impact:

  • $150 million TxDOT investment
  • Completion expected 2027
  • 15-20 minute commute time reduction
  • Enhanced safety and traffic flow

The Smithville Business & Technology Park

Breaking ground in Q2 2025, this 200-acre development will bring an estimated 2,500 jobs to the area. Already, three major companies have signed letters of intent, including a renewable energy manufacturer and a logistics distribution center serving Central Texas.

Downtown Revitalization Initiative

The city's $12 million downtown improvement project includes:

  • Historic building restoration incentives
  • New mixed-use development opportunities
  • Expanded riverside walk and park system
  • Infrastructure upgrades supporting new restaurants and retail

Infrastructure Investments Setting the Foundation

Water and Utilities

Smithville's new $30 million water treatment facility, operational since 2024, can support a population of 25,000—nearly three times the current population. This forward-thinking infrastructure means new developments like The Prairie Smithville won't face the water restrictions plaguing other fast-growing Texas communities.

Fiber Optic Network

The city's partnership with a regional internet provider brings gigabit fiber to 90% of Smithville by 2026, making it a viable option for remote workers and tech professionals who need reliable, high-speed connectivity.

Tech Infrastructure Advantage:

Gigabit City status since 2013 + 90% gigabit fiber coverage by 2026 = Perfect for remote workers and tech professionals

Education Expansion

Smithville ISD's passed bond includes:

  • New elementary school (opening 2026)
  • High school expansion and STEM center
  • Athletic facility upgrades
  • Technology improvements district-wide

Smart Growth, Not Just Fast Growth

Unlike boom towns that grow without planning, Smithville's development follows a comprehensive master plan that preserves:

  • The historic downtown character
  • Access to natural areas like Buescher State Park
  • Community gathering spaces
  • Reasonable traffic flow and parking

The city's development code encourages walkable neighborhoods, mixed-use developments, and green space preservation—ensuring Smithville remains livable as it grows.

What This Means for Property Values

Historical data from similar Texas towns shows property values typically increase 25-35% in the five years following major infrastructure investments like those happening in Smithville. Early investors in communities like Georgetown, Pflugerville, and Cedar Park saw even greater returns as those cities matured.

Current median home prices in Smithville remain 40% below Austin's, but that gap is expected to narrow as development accelerates. The Prairie Smithville offers buyers the opportunity to lock in today's prices before the market adjusts to reflect the area's true growth potential.

The Corporate Migration Factor

As major employers continue relocating operations to Texas, they're looking beyond Austin's city limits for affordable land and willing workforces. Smithville's strategic location along Highway 71, between Austin and Houston, makes it attractive for:

  • Distribution and logistics companies
  • Manufacturing operations
  • Back-office corporate functions
  • Tech company satellite offices

Each new employer brings jobs, which brings families, which drives demand for quality housing.

Retail and Entertainment Following Rooftops

National retailers follow a simple formula: rooftops equal revenue. As Smithville's population grows, expect to see:

  • National restaurant chains joining local favorites
  • Expanded retail options reducing trips to Austin
  • Entertainment venues and family activities
  • Healthcare facilities and specialized services

Early conversations with developers suggest a major retail center is planned for the Highway 71 corridor, though official announcements are pending.
